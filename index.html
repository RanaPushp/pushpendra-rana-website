About Me

I am an interdisciplinary AI and social scientist with a background in natural sciences, social sciences, statistics, and machine learning. My research lies at the intersection of Responsible AI, sociotechnical system design, and causal evaluation, with a focus on how AI systems are measured, interpreted, contested, and governed in real-world contexts.

My work focuses on using AI and experimental methods to study complex social-ecological systems and to design robust, equitable, and accountable AI-enabled governance solutions that strengthen environmental resilience, community livelihoods, and climate adaptation through iterative co-learning between human and AI systems.

I combine rigorous academic research with 15+ years of leadership in the Indian Forest Service, where I have managed complex human–forest interactions and real-world governance challenges. I hold a Ph.D. in Geography from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, with a specialization in Human Geography and a minor in Statistical Methods. My postdoctoral work spans the University of Illinois, NC State University, and the Carolina Population Center at UNC Chapel Hill.

Research Interests

My overall goal is to build Responsible AI that supports decision-making in high-stakes sociotechnical systems by explicitly integrating feasibility bounds, institutional and incentive-aware system design, and causally rigorous evaluation.
